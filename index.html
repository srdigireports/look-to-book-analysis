⚠️ Data Accuracy Disclaimer — Please Read

This report covers the initial post-migration window for both properties. During booking engine transitions, industry standard practice acknowledges a data variance margin of ± 10–15% due to the following factors:

Tag overlap — Both AZDS and Namastay tracking events fired simultaneously during the cutover window, potentially inflating click counts.
Page path gaps — Conversions from homepage or non-property-specific URLs may not be captured by current page path filters.
Tracking latency — Slight delays in event activation during the initial rollout of Namastay tracking may result in minor gaps between click and purchase data.
Cross-session attribution — Users who clicked to the booking engine in one session but purchased in another may not be fully attributed.
All figures should be treated as directional indicators rather than absolute values. Google recommends a 2–4 week data stabilization window post-migration. Even at the conservative end of the margin (−15%), all key trends remain positive.

Key Takeaways — Hotel June West LA
01
L2B jumped 32% under Namastay
Conversion rate rose from 12.92% to 17.05% — a 4.13 percentage point improvement. For every 100 users entering the booking engine, Namastay converts 4 more into purchasers than AZDS did.
02
+$105.8K incremental revenue
Revenue surged 51.8% from $204.2K to $309.9K. This is the single largest PoP revenue gain across the current migration portfolio, driven by both higher purchase volume and higher average booking values.
03
Average booking value up $151.73
ABV increased 29% from $523.52 to $675.25, suggesting Namastay’s booking flow may be better at surfacing room upgrades, add-ons, or longer-stay bookings compared to AZDS.
04
Fewer clicks, more purchases
Click volume dropped 10.8% but purchases rose 17.7%. This pattern indicates higher-intent traffic is entering the Namastay booking engine — fewer window-shoppers, more bookers.
